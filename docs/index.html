🌲 Pacific Yew Habitat Map

45 study areas across BC's Coastal Western Hemlock (CWH) zone.
XGBoost model · 10 m GEE satellite embeddings · 2024 imagery.

About

Predicted probability of Pacific yew (Taxus brevifolia) habitat using XGBoost trained on iNaturalist observations and Google Earth Engine satellite embeddings. Each tile covers ~10×10 km.

Use the observation panel (top right) to report yew sightings and improve the model.

    🪓 Logging Impact on Yew Habitat

    Estimated yew habitat destroyed by logging in each BEC subzone, based on yew prevalence in surviving old-growth forest (P≥0.5). Covers 99 of 100 tiles (~9,900 km²) across the CWH, ICH, and other zones.

    Methodology:
    1. Yew prevalence rate = mean yew probability in old-growth (>150 yr) pixels (continuous, not a threshold)
    2. Est. original yew = mean_prob × (old-growth + all logged area) × 0.01 ha/pixel
    3. Current yew = sum of all pixel probabilities after suppression × 0.01 ha/pixel
    4. Destroyed (logging) = original − current (forests <150 yr assumed logged, prob → 0)
    5. Logging suppression: all forests <150 yr → ×0 (assumed logged); >150 yr → ×1
    6. Elevation suppression: linear ×0 at 0 m → ×1 at 30 m (Copernicus GLO-30 DEM; suppresses low-coastal habitat)
    7. Fire suppression = probability mass lost by fire-date modifier [(2024−fire_year)/124]
    8. Data: XGBoost model on 2024 GEE embeddings, BC VRI 2024, BC BEC zones, BC Historical Fire Perimeters

    🔥 Fire Impact on Yew Habitat

    Yew probability is scaled by a linear fire-recovery modifier: 0 for a fire in 2024 (complete suppression) → 1 for a fire in 1900 (full recovery). Formula: (2024 − fire_year) / 124.

    ⚠️ Lower mainland tiles (Stave Lake, Chilliwack Uplands): an additional −0.2 suppression is applied outside protected areas to correct for logging activity not captured by VRI stand-age data.
